# The Aurora Crew — Character Art Direction v1

**Purpose:** Define a cohesive but highly distinguishable visual system for the eight crew members of `The Aurora` before generating final character art.

---

## 1. Shared Art Direction

## World style

**Luxury nautical editorial with adventurous pirate energy**

- elegant rather than grimy
- feminine, confident, lightly sensual
- practical enough to belong on a ship
- original designs, not direct references to existing anime characters
- refined illustration style, memorable silhouettes, premium finish

## Shared visual anchors

To keep the team cohesive:

- navy, ivory, and antique gold appear somewhere in every design
- each crew member also owns one dominant accent color
- nautical details repeat subtly: rope knots, compass motifs, brass hardware, sailor collars, belts, boots, scarves, shell or star charms
- outfits may show shoulder, waist, leg, or neckline, but should read as **stylish and capable**, not costume-only fan service
- silhouettes must remain readable even in small dashboard cards

## Diversity target

The crew should visibly vary across:

- ethnicity
- skin tone
- face shape
- hair texture
- body type
- height impression
- silhouette
- styling era

The goal is that a viewer can identify each woman instantly from:

1. outline
2. palette
3. one signature accessory
4. role-specific prop

# 5. Composition Guidance

## Group shot hierarchy

- Robin near the route table or center-left leadership position
- Mia elevated, looking outward
- Zoe in motion near charts
- Bella near written scrolls
- Lila with camera/sketching tools
- Nora inspecting cargo or documents
- Roxy near sails/wind instruments
- Emma engaging with letters or message bottles

## Posing rule

Each pose should communicate role before beauty:

- Mia scans
- Zoe invents
- Bella writes
- Lila frames
- Nora verifies
- Roxy directs timing
- Emma welcomes
- Robin commands

---

# 6. Dashboard Asset Recommendations

## Required assets

For each crew member:

1. full-body character sheet
2. bust portrait
3. square avatar crop
4. transparent cutout if possible

## Card-level recognizability

At avatar size, preserve:

- dominant accent color
- unique hair shape
- one visible accessory
- distinct neckline / collar / hat shape

---

# 7. Final Cohesion Check

Before approving final art, verify:

- no two characters share the same hair silhouette
- no two characters rely on the same accent palette
- every role is readable without labels
- the team feels like one crew, not eight unrelated pin-ups
- sensuality never overrides professionalism or function
- the visual system remains original to NayzFreedom
